What this is about

Data sovereignty, not just as a slogan.

Schrems II changed the rules of the game in 2020. The EU Data Act now applies. Swiss insurers demand no-US-provider clauses. The 2026 cloud discussion isn't "where is it cheapest?", it's "where do our data actually stay?". We help you answer that question cleanly on the technical side.

What we do specifically: we build and run cloud setups on EU providers. Hetzner, Infomaniak, Gcore, OVH, IONOS - all with our own hands-on experience. We migrate your existing setup away from US hyperscalers, plan multi-cloud strategies, build disaster recovery, harden security setups, optimise costs.

What sets us apart from cloud advisors: we run our own EU stack. Hetzner Nuremberg/Helsinki for apps and databases, Infomaniak Geneva as Swiss witness, IONOS Frankfurt for backups, Gcore Luxembourg for edge/WAF. What we recommend to you, we run ourselves - not from PowerPoint, from practice.

Cost optimisation

Migration from US hyperscaler to Hetzner often saves 30-50% at the same (or better) performance. We do before/after calculations with ROI numbers.

EU providers we run
Four primary providers, depending on the requirement.
We don't recommend "the one provider", we recommend the one that fits your requirements. Here are the four primary providers we work with daily.

Hetzner Online GmbH

Nuremberg / Helsinki · Germany / Finland

Our default for application and database servers. ISO/IEC 27001 certified, excellent price/performance, EU data protection, fast support. We run our own infrastructure there.

Infomaniak Network SA

Geneva · Switzerland

When you need Swiss data residency. ISO/IEC 27001 certified, Swiss-made cloud, no US group affiliation. We use Infomaniak for witness servers and the VPN control plane.

Gcore S.à r.l.

Luxembourg · EU

Edge/WAF protection and CDN. Anti-DDoS, bot protection, DNS. Edge locations restricted to EU regions. For enterprise setups with BSI requirements, alternative EU providers are factored into the setup.

OVHcloud / IONOS

France / Germany

For specific requirements: OVH for health data (ISO 27018), IONOS for external backups (DAX-listed parent group), both GDPR-compliant and EU-sovereign.

GDPR as standard, project-individual choice possible. Our own infrastructure and our own products run fully GDPR-compliant on EU providers.
