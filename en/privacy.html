2. Legal Bases

As a Swiss company we are subject to the Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection (FADP/revFADP). For users from the EU/EEA, the provisions of the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) additionally apply. We process personal data on the following legal bases:

  • Performance of a contract (Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR / Art. 31(1) FADP)
  • Legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR / Art. 31(1) FADP) — in particular IT security and operational integrity
  • Legal obligation (Art. 6(1)(c) GDPR) — in particular accounting and retention obligations
  • Consent (Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR / Art. 31(1) FADP) — where expressly given

3. Data Collected

When visiting our website, the web server automatically records the following information (server log files):

  • Browser type and version
  • Operating system used
  • Referrer URL
  • IP address (anonymised after 30 days)
  • Time of the server request

This data is not merged with other data sources and is automatically deleted after 30 days. It is evaluated solely for the purpose of IT security and error diagnosis.

4. Hosting & Data Location

The infrastructure of RC-Tech Solutions is operated geo-redundantly within the European Union. Primary application and database servers: Hetzner Online GmbH, data centres in Nuremberg (Germany) and Helsinki (Finland). An additional witness server for high availability and VPN control plane is operated at Infomaniak Network SA in Geneva (Switzerland).

External, encrypted backups are kept at IONOS SE in Frankfurt am Main (Germany; parent group United Internet AG, DAX-listed). Edge/WAF protection (anti-DDoS, bot protection) is provided via Gcore S.à r.l. (Luxembourg), with edge locations restricted to EU regions.

The principal infrastructure providers (Hetzner Online GmbH, Infomaniak Network SA, IONOS SE) are ISO/IEC 27001 certified. For AI inference, AWS Europe SARL (also ISO/IEC 27001 certified) is used in addition; for details see clause 6.3. Other sub-processors comply with industry-standard security levels with documented certifications or equivalent technical and organisational measures. There is no primary transfer of personal data to third countries outside the EU/EEA, except for the sub-processors mentioned in clause 6.

5. Data Processing on Behalf for Our Own SaaS Products

Within our own SaaS products, in particular the industry solution Easy & Smart Booking, we process personal data on behalf of our clients (data processing on behalf pursuant to Art. 28 GDPR / Art. 9 FADP). A separate data processing agreement (DPA) is concluded with each client.

The data processing covers:

  • Hosting and storage of client data and end-customer data (tenants)
  • Technical monitoring and logging for operations and security
  • Automated backups and recovery services
  • DNS administration and SSL/TLS certificate management

All data is processed exclusively on servers in the EU (DE, FI) and Switzerland. We have no substantive access to end-customer data and act solely on the instructions of the respective client.

7. SSL/TLS Encryption

All our websites and platforms use TLS 1.2 / 1.3 encryption. HSTS (HTTP Strict Transport Security) is enabled on all domains. There are no unencrypted endpoints.

This website uses no tracking cookies and no third-party analytics services (no Google Analytics, Meta Pixel, etc.). Only strictly necessary cookies are used (e.g. session cookies for logged-in users, language settings). These cookies do not require consent as they are strictly necessary for the operation of the website.

9. Contact

If you contact us by e-mail or via a contact form, your details are stored to process the enquiry. Legal basis: performance of a contract or pre-contractual measures (Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR) or legitimate interest (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR). We do not pass on this data without your consent. Contact data is deleted as soon as the enquiry has been finally dealt with, and at the latest after 12 months unless a contractual relationship exists.

10. Data Security (TOM)

Scope: The technical and organisational measures (TOM) below apply to our productive SaaS platforms and back-end systems in which personal client data is processed (in particular EasyAndSmartBooking). The website rc-tech-solutions.com itself is a static information offering without a database back-end; for that website, TLS/HSTS (see clause 7) and server log anonymisation (see clause 12) apply.

On the productive SaaS platforms we apply the following measures:

  • AES-256-GCM envelope encryption for sensitive fields (API keys, credentials, selected CRM data)
  • bcrypt hashing for passwords (12 rounds)
  • SSH key-based server access (no password login)
  • Role-based access control (RBAC) with audit logging
  • Automated daily backups with encrypted storage (rolling 30-day retention)
  • Firewall with whitelist principle and DDoS protection via edge provider
  • Fail2Ban against brute-force attacks
  • Regular security updates and vulnerability scans

Detailed information on our TOM is provided as part of a data processing agreement (DPA) or upon specific request.

11. Your Rights

You have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right of access (Art. 15 GDPR / Art. 25 FADP)
  • Right to rectification (Art. 16 GDPR / Art. 32(1) FADP)
  • Right to erasure (Art. 17 GDPR / Art. 32(2)(c) FADP) — right to be forgotten
  • Right to restriction (Art. 18 GDPR)
  • Right to data portability (Art. 20 GDPR / Art. 28 FADP) — right to receive data in a machine-readable format
  • Right to object (Art. 21 GDPR)
  • Right to withdraw consent (Art. 7(3) GDPR) — at any time with effect for the future

To exercise your rights, please contact us at: datenschutz@rc-tech-solutions.com

12. Retention Periods

We store personal data differentiated by data category and only for as long as necessary for the respective purpose or as required by statutory retention periods:

  • Server logs: 30 days, then anonymised or deleted
  • Contact enquiries: up to 12 months unless a contractual relationship exists
  • Operational client data: for the duration of the contract plus a 30-day grace period for retrieval; thereafter deleted
  • Backup snapshots (encrypted at IONOS Frankfurt): rolling for a maximum of 30 days; older backups are automatically overwritten. Backups serve solely for restoration in the event of an incident, not for long-term archiving
  • Accounting-relevant records (invoices, payment metadata, tax records): 10 years pursuant to Art. 958f Swiss Code of Obligations. These records are kept in our accounting — not in the operational platform database — and contain only contract- and payment-related data
  • AI inference: AWS Bedrock does not persistently store prompts and responses pursuant to the AWS agreement; no personal AI inference logs are retained

13. Right to Lodge a Complaint

You have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority if you consider that the processing of your data violates applicable law.

  • Switzerland: Federal Data Protection and Information Commissioner (FDPIC), edoeb.admin.ch
  • EU: the competent data protection authority of your country of residence

14. Use of Automated Systems and AI (EU AI Act)

RC-Tech Solutions uses AI-based tools along three clearly separated paths that are subject to different GDPR scenarios. Important for delineation: RC-Tech acts on the one hand as a producer of its own SaaS products (e.g. EasyAndSmartBooking as an in-house product — see clause 5), and on the other hand as a service provider for client projects (consulting, software development, AI integration). The respective AI architecture differs accordingly.

14.1 Internal Development Tools (Pair Programming)

For the provision of software development and consulting services, RC-Tech uses AI-based tools, in particular Claude Code by Anthropic PBC (548 Market Street PMB 90375, San Francisco, CA 94104, USA) as a pair-programming tool. Only RC-Tech's own code base, documentation and architectural concepts are processed in this context. Confidential client data is not transmitted to this development AI. All AI-generated work products are reviewed, tested and released manually before delivery to the client. For consulting or review engagements where AI assistance with client data is appropriate, the data transfer is agreed with the client in advance and governed contractually. The legal basis for any third-country transfer occurring in this context is primarily the EU standard contractual clauses (SCCs) pursuant to Implementing Decision (EU) 2021/914, supplemented by a transfer impact assessment. Where Anthropic maintains a valid certification under the EU–US Data Privacy Framework (EU Commission adequacy decision of 10 July 2023), this may serve as an additional legal basis; if the adequacy decision lapses, the SCCs remain the primary basis.

14.2 AI Features within RC-Tech's Own SaaS Platforms (EasyAndSmartBooking)

For RC-Tech's own SaaS products, in particular EasyAndSmartBooking as an in-house product (booking chatbot, translations into four target languages (EN/FR/IT/ES) from German, CRM profile summaries with pseudonymisation, admin support with PII sanitiser, review moderation, AI-assisted text generation as well as the upcoming voice agent), a deliberate, uniform AI architecture has been chosen: Anthropic PBC's Claude models obtained via AWS Bedrock in the EU region eu-central-1 (Frankfurt am Main). Contractual partner: AWS Europe SARL (Luxembourg). AI inference takes place entirely within the AWS EU region — data does not leave the EU/EEA territory. Anthropic PBC acts as a sub-processor via AWS Bedrock without any direct data flow to the USA.

Data minimisation pursuant to Art. 5(1)(c) GDPR is implemented at code level (first-name reduction, pseudonymisation in CRM profiles, exclusion of allergy information from chatbot prompts, pre-save sanitiser in the admin support chat). End customers can deactivate the AI chatbot via opt-out (right to object pursuant to Art. 21 GDPR). AI features are provided exclusively via the platform configuration of the provider — tenants cannot supply their own AI API keys.

14.3 AI Integration in Client Projects (Consulting)

For client projects with AI integration scope (see T&C section 3), the choice of model and hosting is project-individual. RC-Tech generally prefers Claude via AWS Bedrock EU but, depending on the use case and the client's compliance requirements, also chooses other providers (e.g. Azure OpenAI Service in EU regions, Google Vertex AI europe-west, or local/open-source models on client infrastructure). The specific AI architecture, data flows, sub-processors and legal bases are governed bindingly in the respective project-specific data processing agreement (DPA) and in the client's data protection documentation — not in this Privacy Policy. This Privacy Policy describes only the data processing carried out by RC-Tech itself, not client projects.

14.4 Transparency under Art. 50 of the EU AI Act

End customers are clearly informed in every chatbot interaction that they are communicating with an automated system (transparency obligation under Art. 50 of Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 (EU AI Act)). The booking chatbot is functionally limited to appointment booking and information about services and expressly does not provide any medical, health-related, diagnostic or therapeutic advice. It does not qualify as a high-risk AI system within the meaning of Annex III of the AI Act.

No automated decision-making with legal effect within the meaning of Art. 22 GDPR takes place. The CRM profile summary (customer brain) provides pseudonymised recommendations to salon staff — the use is by staff as humans-in-the-loop, no automated decision.

Where additional AI models, sub-processors or new application areas are introduced, these will be documented in advance in this Privacy Policy and in the sub-processor list. Material changes to the data protection setup will be communicated to clients pursuant to clause 15 with reasonable lead time; the right of objection on changes to sub-processors remains unaffected.
