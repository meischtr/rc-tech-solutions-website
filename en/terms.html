1. Scope

These General Terms and Conditions (T&C) govern the business relationship between RC-Tech Solutions GmbH, Via Montalin 13, 7013 Domat/Ems, Switzerland (hereinafter «RC-Tech» or «Provider») and its clients (hereinafter «Client»).

They apply to all services, in particular software development, cloud engineering, AI integration as well as compliance & data protection.

Geographical scope: RC-Tech does not serve clients domiciled or incorporated in the United States of America (USA). Orders or enquiries from the USA will not be accepted; no contractual relationship shall come into existence in such cases.

Deviating terms of the Client are not recognised unless RC-Tech expressly agrees to their applicability in writing.

2. Conclusion of Contract

The contract is concluded by written order confirmation from RC-Tech, by execution of an individual project or maintenance agreement, or by registration on one of our platforms and acceptance of these T&C.

Offers by RC-Tech are non-binding unless expressly marked otherwise.

3. Scope of Services

The specific scope of services is set out in the respective individual contract (offer, project agreement, maintenance agreement, SaaS agreement).

Typical service areas:

  • Software development: Web applications, APIs, SaaS products (full-stack, TypeScript). Including discovery/architecture, MVP and platform development as well as ongoing maintenance pursuant to the individual contract.
  • Cloud engineering: Server architecture, deployments, monitoring, security, migration to EU providers. Including managed hosting on our own infrastructure as well as ongoing operations (patches, incident response, SLA) pursuant to the individual contract.
  • AI integration: Integration of large language models (LLMs) into existing workflows, prompt engineering, data protection architecture. The choice of model and hosting (e.g. AWS Bedrock EU, Azure OpenAI EU, Google Vertex AI EU or local/open-source models) is determined on a project-individual basis according to use case and the Client's compliance requirements. RC-Tech's own SaaS products primarily use AWS Bedrock in the EU region eu-central-1; for client projects this choice is not mandatory.
  • Compliance & data protection: revFADP and GDPR audits, EU AI Act compliance reviews, drafting and maintenance of data processing agreements (DPAs), documentation of technical and organisational measures (TOM), records of processing activities, privacy-by-design reviews, Switzerland-EU cross-border assessments.

4. Prices and Payment

The prices agreed in the individual contract or in the current price list shall apply. All prices are exclusive of statutory value added tax unless stated otherwise.

Invoicing is carried out, depending on the contract type:

  • Fixed-price projects: by milestones or upon acceptance
  • Time and material: monthly invoicing at hourly or daily rates agreed on a project-individual basis pursuant to the individual contract or current offer
  • SaaS / hosting / maintenance: monthly or annually in advance

Payment term: 30 days net from invoice date. In the event of payment default, default interest of 5% pursuant to Art. 104 of the Swiss Code of Obligations (CO) shall be owed.

RC-Tech reserves the right to adjust recurring prices with 60 days' notice prior to the end of the current billing period.

5. Term and Termination

Project agreements end upon acceptance of the agreed deliverables.

Maintenance and SaaS agreements are concluded for an indefinite term unless a fixed term is agreed. Both parties may terminate with 30 days' notice prior to the end of the current billing period (month or year).

The right to extraordinary termination for cause remains unaffected. Cause is given in particular in the event of substantial payment default, serious breach of contract or unreasonable changes to the circumstances of use.

6. Client's Duties to Cooperate

The Client shall reasonably cooperate in the performance of the contract, in particular by:

  • Timely provision of required information, access credentials and data
  • Designating a responsible contact person with decision-making authority
  • Timely review and acceptance of intermediate and final results
  • Confidential handling of access credentials

Delays caused by lack of cooperation are at the Client's expense.

7. Data Protection and Data Processing

RC-Tech processes personal data in accordance with the Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection (FADP, revised version 2023) and the EU GDPR. For details see the Privacy Policy.

Where RC-Tech processes personal data on behalf of the Client (in particular for hosting and SaaS services), the parties shall enter into a separate Data Processing Agreement (DPA) pursuant to Art. 28 GDPR or Art. 9 FADP.

The primary infrastructure is operated geo-redundantly within the European Union: Hetzner Nuremberg (DE) and Helsinki (FI) as main locations, Infomaniak Geneva (CH) as witness, IONOS Frankfurt (DE) for external backups, Gcore Luxembourg for edge/WAF protection. Individual AI-based functions may involve sub-processors in third countries (see clause 14); the respective transfer mechanisms are documented in the Privacy Policy.

8. Intellectual Property and Rights of Use

8.1 Pre-existing rights (background IP). All rights to software, source code, platforms, modules and tools that RC-Tech has developed independently, regardless of any specific assignment or prior to the start of the specific project, remain with RC-Tech. This includes in particular reusable architectural building blocks, internal libraries, best-practice templates and the know-how derived therefrom.

8.2 Rights to project-specific deliverables. All rights to deliverables developed specifically for the Client remain with RC-Tech in principle. Depending on the model agreed, the Client receives different rights of use:

  • (a) Standard — non-exclusive right of use. Unless otherwise agreed, upon full payment the Client receives a perpetual, transferable, non-exclusive right of use to the deliverables created for it. The right of use covers use in the Client's own business operations, internal modification and operation by commissioned IT service providers. RC-Tech retains the right to reuse general concepts, methods, architectural patterns and non-project-specific code for other clients.
  • (b) Exclusive licence (against surcharge). By separate agreement, RC-Tech may undertake not to develop substantially comparable solutions for competitors of the Client for an agreed period (typically 12 to 24 months). The specific industry or competitive scope as well as the term shall be defined in the individual contract.
  • (c) Transfer of copyright (against surcharge). By separate agreement, the transferable copyrights (Art. 16 of the Swiss Copyright Act, CopA) or the exclusive rights of exploitation in the project-specific code may be fully transferred to the Client. In this case, RC-Tech waives the reuse of these project-specific components. A transfer of pre-existing rights pursuant to clause 8.1 is not included. To the extent that RC-Tech is to continue providing maintenance of the solution, the individual contract shall provide for a corresponding code access right in favour of RC-Tech.

8.3 Client content. Content contributed by the Client itself (texts, data, trademark rights, designs, logos) remains the property of the Client. RC-Tech receives a right of use solely for the purpose of providing the contractually owed service.

8.4 Open-source components. To the extent that RC-Tech uses open-source components in deliverables, the respective open-source licences alone shall apply to such components. RC-Tech will provide information on the components used and their licences upon request.

9. Warranty

RC-Tech provides services professionally and with the care of an experienced IT service provider. In the event of defects, RC-Tech has the right and obligation to remedy them within a reasonable period.

The Client shall report defects in writing without delay. Obvious defects must be notified within 30 days of acceptance.

10. Liability

In the case of slight negligence, RC-Tech is liable only for breach of material contractual obligations and limited to the foreseeable damage typical for the contract. Liability is in any case limited to the net fees paid by the Client in the last 12 months.

Due care in selecting service providers: RC-Tech selects and monitors sub-processors and other service providers with the diligence of an experienced IT provider. Where personal data is concerned, RC-Tech is liable for its sub-processors as for its own conduct pursuant to Art. 28 GDPR and Art. 101 CO — in each case within the limits of liability set out in this clause.

RC-Tech is, however, not liable for:

  • Force majeure (natural events, pandemics, large-scale cyber attacks on internet infrastructure, governmental orders)
  • Outages or defects of individual third-party providers (in particular Hetzner, IONOS, Infomaniak, Gcore, AWS Europe SARL including the Anthropic PBC AI models obtained via AWS Bedrock, Twilio, fiskaly, Google Ireland Ltd., Microsoft Ireland Ltd., Meta Platforms Ireland Ltd. and other SMS and e-mail providers), to the extent that such occur despite careful selection and monitoring and lie outside the sphere of influence of RC-Tech
  • Independent contractual relationships between the Client and third-party providers (in particular Stripe Payments Europe Ltd. for payment processing; see Privacy Policy clause 6.2)
  • Improper use by the Client, including breach of duties to cooperate or duties of care

For the loss of data, RC-Tech is liable only to the extent that the damage would also have occurred if the Client had performed proper data backups.

11. Confidentiality

Both parties undertake to keep the other party's confidential information secret during the term of the contract and for 3 years thereafter. Excluded is information that is publicly available or has been lawfully obtained from a third party.

Upon request, the parties shall enter into a separate NDA.

12. Reference Naming

RC-Tech may name the Client (company name, logo) as a reference in marketing materials, on the website and in pitches after acceptance of the project, unless the Client objects in writing. Confidential project content will not be disclosed.

13. Subcontractors and Sub-processors

RC-Tech is entitled to engage subcontractors for the performance of its services. RC-Tech remains fully responsible for the proper provision of services.

Where personal data is processed on behalf of the Client (data processing on behalf pursuant to Art. 28 GDPR or Art. 9 FADP), the engagement and replacement of sub-processors shall take place in accordance with the respective Data Processing Agreement (DPA). RC-Tech shall inform the Client in advance of any planned changes to the list of sub-processors and grant the Client the opportunity to object within a reasonable period.

14. Use of AI Tools and Automated Systems

RC-Tech uses AI-based tools along three clearly separated paths that are subject to different data protection regimes:

14.1 Internal development tools (pair programming). For the provision of software development and consulting services, RC-Tech uses AI-based tools, in particular models from Anthropic PBC (Claude Code as a pair-programming tool). Confidential client data is in principle not transferred to external AI services in the development process. All AI-generated work products are reviewed, tested and released manually before delivery to the Client.

14.2 AI features within RC-Tech's own SaaS products. AI-based product features within the SaaS platforms operated by RC-Tech (e.g. the booking chatbot on EasyAndSmartBooking as an in-house product) are provided via AWS Bedrock in the EU region eu-central-1 (AWS Europe SARL, Luxembourg) as a deliberate architectural decision. Anthropic acts as a sub-processor via AWS without any direct data flow to the USA. For details see the Privacy Policy clause 14. The systems concerned comply with the transparency obligations under Art. 50 of Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 (EU AI Act).

14.3 AI integration in client projects. For client projects with AI integration scope (see clause 3), the choice of model and hosting is project-individual — depending on use case and the Client's compliance requirements, options include AWS Bedrock EU, Azure OpenAI Service in EU regions, Google Vertex AI europe-west or local/open-source models on Client infrastructure. The specific architecture, data flows and sub-processors shall be governed bindingly in the respective individual contract and the project-specific DPA.

RC-Tech does not process special categories of personal data within the meaning of Art. 9 GDPR as part of its own AI-based features. No high-risk AI within the meaning of Annex III of the AI Act is involved.

RC-Tech reserves the right to amend these T&C with 60 days' notice. Changes shall be communicated to the Client by e-mail. If the Client does not object to the changes within 30 days of receipt, they shall be deemed accepted. The right to object shall be specifically pointed out in the change notification.
